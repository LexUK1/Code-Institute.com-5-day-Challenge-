German Bratwurst

Enjoy the authentic taste of Germany with delicious Bratwursts! You'll love these world famous German sausages once you try them, and here we teach you how to make them for your family to enjoy!

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 1/2 pounds boneless pork shoulder
  • 12 ounces pork back fat
  • For the Spice Mixture:
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons freshly ground white pepper
  • 3/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground mace
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon dried marjoram
  • 3/4 teaspoon mustard powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed caraway seeds
  • 32mm natural hog casing

PREPARATION

  1. Combine the pork, fat and crushed ice in a bowl and then, working quickly, use a meat grinder to grind the mixture through a 1/4 inch (6mm) die. Note: It is imperative that the meat be at a constant cold temperature so that the fat doesn't get too soft.
  2. In a small bowl combine all the spices: Remove the ground meat from the fridge and place it on the stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment. Add the spice mix and dry milk powder. Mix the meat mixture with the paddle for 3-4 minutes until threads begin to appear in the meat: .
  3. Thread your sausage stuffer with the prepared hog casings, fill the sausage stuffer with the meat mixture, and stuff the casings being careful to avoid air gaps while also being careful to not over-stuff the casings. Twist the sausages into links. Use a sausage pricker to prick any air bubbles out of the links.
  4. To prepare the bratwursts, gently poach them in lightly salted water (place sausages in pot of water, gradually bring liquid to a light simmer, and poach sausages until partially cooked, 3-4 minutes) and then fry or grill them. Once poached they will keep in the fridge, tightly wrapped, for up to a week.
  5. Enjoy!
